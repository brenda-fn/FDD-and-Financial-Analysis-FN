FN Assessment: At median performance, Scenthound generates a viable but modestly tight return for a single unit with SBA financing. The investment case improves significantly at 4+ units where the development fee discount reduces per-unit acquisition cost by ~40%, shared operational overhead improves margins, and combined NOI provides strong debt service coverage. The recurring membership revenue model provides more predictable cash flow for debt service than transactional concepts — a meaningful structural advantage.

Green Flags & Red Flags
✓ Green Flags
  • Zero terminations in 3 years across a growing system — rare and significant
  • Recurring membership revenue ($40–$60/month) supports debt service predictability
  • 4-unit development agreement reduces per-unit fee by 40% — economically optimal for multi-unit operators
  • 13.8% median NOI is strong relative to industry benchmarks for service franchises
  • Profitable franchisor with clean audit and no going concern notes
  • Semi-absentee ownership explicitly permitted with GM structure
  • Rapid and consistent growth (18→34→71 units) signals demand and operational scalability
⚑ Red Flags / Watch Items
  • Bottom quartile NOI of 1.2% ($4K) — wide performance spread; GM quality appears to be the primary differentiator
  • Only 22 of 71 units reported full P&L — data set covers 31% of system; not fully representative
  • No year-over-year same-store sales comparison available in this FDD
  • Base-case single unit DSCR of 1.15x is below SBA preferred 1.25x — may require top-half performance for clean loan approval
  • Renewal terms at franchisor discretion (Item 23) — conditions for renewal are not automatic
  • Territory availability in Leonard's target markets cannot be confirmed from FDD alone
